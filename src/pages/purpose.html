why this exists

purpose

This site was built to make you think about the time you spend scrolling and what it actually costs you.

what this project is

This is a web project exploring the real impact of doomscrolling. It combines research-backed data, personal perspective, and an interactive experience that puts you inside the feed itself. The goal is not to lecture. It is to show you what the loop feels like from the inside and let the data speak for itself.

how it works

The home page presents the statistics. Real numbers from real studies about how much time we lose, what it does to our mental health, our sleep, and our ability to focus. The opinion page is a first-person take on what doomscrolling actually feels like day to day.

The scrolling page is the experience itself. It generates an infinite feed of posts, memes, ads, and commentary that mirrors what a real social media feed looks like. The longer you scroll, the more the site calls you out for it. A timer tracks how long you have been there. It is designed to make you feel the pull and then question why you are still going.

why it matters

Most people know doomscrolling is a problem but do not feel it until they are already deep in the feed. Reading a stat is one thing. Watching a timer count your minutes while you keep scrolling anyway is something else entirely. This project tries to close that gap between knowing and feeling.
